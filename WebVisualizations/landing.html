Weather Data: Vizualization Dashboard

Latitude vs Cloudiness, Temperature, Wind Speed, and Humidity



This analysis visualizes the weather of 500+ cities across the world of varying distance from the equator and creates a representative model of weather across world cities. This analysis utilizes a simple Python library, and the OpenWeatherMap API.


Major Trends: There are strong correlations between latitude and max temperature (F) in both hemispheres. In the Northern Hemisphere there is a strong negative correlation (r = -0.88) between latitude and max temperature (F), as a city's latitude increases the temperature decreases.


In the Southern Hemisphere there is a strong positive correlation (r = 0.70) between latitude and max tempearture (F), as a city's latitude increases the temperature also increases. Cities that are closer to the equator have a visible increase in max temperture (F) while cities that are farther away have a visible decrease in max temperture (F) There are no statistically significant differences in latitude and other weather data, such as humidity, cloudiness and wind speed for both hemispheres.


Dashboard created using HTML5, CSS3, and Bootsrap4
